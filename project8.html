Preservation of Archaeal Lipids in Fossil Methane Seeps

Bachelor’s Thesis – University of Göttingen (2017)

Overview

For my bachelor thesis project I investigated the archaeal lipid biomarkers in fossil methane seep carbonates spanning different thermal maturities across the Phanerozoic era. I successfully identifed autochthonous archaeal biomarkers in the 331-347 mya Visean Iberg deposit (Germany), whcih extend the geologic record of archaea by about 30mya at the time. This project was supervised by Prof. Joachim Reitner and Prof. Jan-Peter Duda

Geological Setting

Three seep carbonate deposits were targeted: Late Eocene Whiskey Creek, USA (34–41 Ma); Late Pennsylvanian Dwyka, Namibia (299–307 Ma); and Visean Iberg, Germany (331–347 Ma), one of the oldest reported methane seeps.

Figure.Methane - seep limestones of the Dwyka (A1-B2) (Late Pennsylvanian: 299-307 mya) and Iberg (C1-D2) (Visean: 331-347 mya) deposits.

Methods

Lipids were extracted with organic solvents and analyzed by gas chromatography–mass spectrometry (GC–MS) and gas chromatography–combustion–isotope ratio mass spectrometry (GC–C–IRMS). Thermal maturity was assessed via CPI/OEP indices and hopane/sterane-based parameters (e.g., Ts/C30, C29Ts/(C29+C29Ts), C29 20S/(20S+20R), C31 22S/(22S+22R), MPI-1).

For GC–MS, analyses employed a Thermo Scientific TRACE 1310 GC with Quantum XLS Ultra MS and a ZB-5MS capillary column under standard splitless conditions; mass spectra were acquired at 70 eV (m/z 50–600).

Key Results

  • Maturity: Whiskey Creek is immature (CPI ~2.13; OEP >1; biological configurations and unsaturated hopanes/steranes), whereas Dwyka and Iberg fall within the oil generative window (e.g., Ts/C30 ≈ 0.46–0.57; C29Ts/(C29+C29Ts) ≈ 0.36–0.41; C29 20S/(20S+20R) ≈ 0.42–0.58).
  • Archaeal markers: Whiskey Creek and Dwyka contain high concentrations of PMI and crocetane with δ13C values between −109‰ and −123‰ (V-PDB), indicating AOM-associated archaea.
  • Iberg: Only trace PMI and TME were detected; δ13C around −30 to −32‰ suggests some methane-derived carbon contributed to archaeal biomass despite higher maturity.

Impact

The study shows that preservation of archaeal lipid biomarkers in extractable sedimentary organic matter is limited by thermal maturity. It also provides biomarker evidence for archaea at the Visean Iberg seep and supports a contribution of AOM to carbonate formation—helping bridge the temporal gap in the geologic record of archaea.
