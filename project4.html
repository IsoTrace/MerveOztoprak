Lake Seeburg Biomarkers

Biomarker & Isotope Trends in a Shallow Lake Setting

Overview

In collaboration with the University of Göttingen, I contributed to a study on Lake Seeburg, assessing biomarker distributions and isotopic trends in shallow lake sediments to reconstruct eutrophication patterns and historical environmental changes.

Figure 1. Sampling cores and biomarker extraction from Lake Seeburg sediment.

Methods

We employed **GC-MS biomarker profiling** to quantify hydrocarbons, sterols, and fatty acids from sediment cores spanning decades. Compound-specific carbon isotopic analysis was integrated to evaluate changes in organic matter sources and diagenetic signatures. Correlation with regional records and eutrophication data allowed assessment of anthropogenic and natural drivers.

Key Results

  • Detected shifts in biomarker assemblages corresponding to nutrient loading over time.
  • Identified isotopic trends that reflect changing inputs of autochthonous vs. allochthonous organic matter.
  • Linked biomarker and isotope data to regional planning and land-use changes affecting lake ecology.

Impact

The Lake Seeburg project demonstrates how combining biomarkers and isotope measurements can reconstruct historical ecological changes in shallow lakes. It provides a framework for environmental monitoring and lake management, especially in eutrophic systems.

Publication

Zeman-Kühnert, S., et al. (2023). *Reconstructing eutrophication trends of a shallow lake using biomarker and isotopic signatures*. Organic Geochemistry. S0146638023000013.
