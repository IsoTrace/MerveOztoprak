Lake Seeburg Biomarkers

Biomarker & Isotope Trends in a Shallow Lake Seeburg

Overview

This study, in collaboration with the University of Göttingen, focused on reconstructing the environmental history of Lake Seeburg. Sediment cores were analyzed for biomarkers and isotopic signatures, providing insights into eutrophication processes, anthropogenic impacts, and natural ecological shifts.

Figure 1. Sediment cores and biomarker extraction workflow at Lake Seeburg.

Methods

Lipid biomarker fractions were isolated and analyzed using GC–MS. Fractions were injected on a Thermo Trace 1310 GC coupled to a Quantum XLS Ultra MS, equipped with a Zebron ZB–5MS column. The GC oven was ramped from 80 °C to 320 °C at 5 °C/min and held for 30 minutes. Electron ionization mass spectra were acquired in full scan mode (70 eV, m/z 50–800). Compound identification relied on published libraries and authentic standards:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}. Complementary isotopic measurements and depth profiling allowed comparison with regional planning records and eutrophication data.

Key Results

  • Depth-dependent shifts in biomarker assemblages aligned with nutrient input history.
  • Carbon isotopic signatures highlighted changing balance between autochthonous and allochthonous sources.
  • Integration with regional records revealed land-use and management decisions as major drivers of ecological change.

Impact

The Lake Seeburg project demonstrates how biomarker and isotopic data can reconstruct shallow-lake ecosystem histories. This integrated approach offers valuable tools for environmental monitoring and management of eutrophic lakes.

Publication: Zeman-Kühnert, S., et al. (2023). Reconstructing eutrophication trends of a shallow lake using biomarker and isotopic signatures. Organic Geochemistry. https://doi.org/10.1016/S0146-6380(23)00001-3
