Discovering Nature’s Fingerprints

Isotope Ratio Analysis on Bioanalytical Mass Spectrometers

Overview

This PhD project focuses on enabling high-precision isotope ratio measurements using soft-ionization mass spectrometry in bioanalytical workflows. By combining LC–MS techniques with isotope-ratio methods, it opens new doors in environmental chemistry, forensics, and molecular tracing.

Figure 1. Bioanalytical Orbitrap mass spectrometer workflow.

Methods

We developed LC–Orbitrap workflows with improved calibration and correction models for isotope ratios. The methods integrated soft-ionization techniques (APCI, ESI) and were benchmarked against traditional IRMS approaches to validate performance.

Key Results

  • Achieved sub-permil reproducibility in compound-specific isotope ratios.
  • Enabled site-specific isotope analysis of complex biomolecules.
  • Demonstrated proof-of-concept applications in forensic casework and metabolic tracing.

Impact

The project demonstrates the feasibility of integrating isotope ratio methods into mainstream bioanalytical platforms, bridging a long-standing gap between traditional isotope geochemistry and applied analytical sciences.
