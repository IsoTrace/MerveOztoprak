Ecotoxicology of Neonicotinoid Pesticides

Environmental Safety Division (Ecotoxicology), BayerCropScience

Overview

As a trainee in the Environmental Safety Division (Ecotoxicology Department) at Bayer CropScience in Monheim, Germany (June–September 2017), I worked on laboratory testing that supports risk assessment for agrochemical products. My main contributions were performing GLP-compliant acute and chronic toxicity bioassays with soil model organisms and developing an automated, image-based evaluation system to accelerate and standardize toxicity readings.

Methods

I executed standard ecotoxicological test designs under GLP, covering both acute and chronic exposure scenarios for soil-dwelling model organisms. To increase analytical throughput and consistency, I implemented an image-based evaluation workflow that captured plate images and processed them with predefined criteria for counting and endpoint scoring, thereby reducing operator bias and turnaround time.

Key Results

  • Completed a series of GLP-compliant tests spanning acute and chronic endpoints on soil model organisms.
  • Designed and presented an automated image-based evaluation approach that improved throughput and data quality for toxicity assessments.

Impact

The experience strengthened my proficiency with GLP workflows and quantitative assessment of biological endpoints. The automation prototype provided a practical path to scale routine bioassays with better reproducibility and faster reporting—skills and practices I continue to apply in analytical method development.
