Bayer CropScience

Isotope and Metabolomics Workflows in Crop Science

Overview

During my time at Bayer CropScience, I contributed to biotechnology and plant metabolomics projects that aimed to improve crop productivity and resilience. My role focused on applying advanced LC–MS/MS workflows and isotope ratio mass spectrometry (IRMS) to characterize plant metabolic pathways and trace isotopic signatures.

Figure 1. LC–MS/MS instrumentation for plant metabolomics at Bayer CropScience.

Methods

I developed and optimized workflows combining **LC–MS/MS targeted metabolomics** with **compound-specific isotope analysis**. These workflows enabled identification of key metabolites under stress conditions, while IRMS measurements provided insights into nutrient assimilation and isotopic labeling experiments. Collaboration across multidisciplinary teams ensured integration with genomics and agronomic field trials.

Key Results

  • Enabled high-throughput LC–MS/MS screening of plant metabolites across multiple genotypes.
  • Applied isotope tracing to assess nitrogen and carbon fluxes in plant metabolic networks.
  • Contributed analytical expertise to biotech projects targeting crop resilience and yield improvement.

Impact

This work highlighted the potential of combining metabolomics with isotope workflows to advance biotechnology research in agriculture. It provided Bayer CropScience with tools to evaluate plant physiology at the molecular level, accelerating the development of sustainable crop solutions.
