Australian National University Project

Isotopic & Biomarker Reconstructions of Precambrian Ecosystems

Overview

During my tenure at ANU, I conducted fieldwork in Western Australia, sampling Precambrian core fragments. The aim was to reconstruct early microbial ecosystems using combined **isotope ratio measurements** and **biomarker analysis**.

Figure 1. Precambrian core sample collection and processing.

Methods

I applied compound-specific isotope ratio analysis (CS-IRMS / LC–MS where applicable) paired with biomarker GC–MS profiling. We targeted hopanes, steranes, and carotenoids preserved in ancient sediments, and measured isotopic signatures across these molecules. Calibrations and correction models were integrated to account for diagenetic alteration and instrument drift.

Key Results

  • Resolved biomarker distributions consistent with microbial community reconstructions in Archaean strata.
  • Observed isotopic offsets between lipid biomarkers suggesting metabolic heterogeneity.
  • Linked isotope–biomarker evidence to paleoenvironments (e.g. redox gradients, nutrient availability).

Impact

This work bridges molecular geochemistry and stable isotope science, providing a window into Earth’s earliest ecosystems. The approach demonstrates how combining biomarker chemistry with isotopes can reconstruct ancient metabolism, community structure, and environmental conditions.
