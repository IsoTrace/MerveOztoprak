CUHK – Hong Kong

LC–Orbitrap Workflows for Metabolomics

Overview

In collaboration with the **Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK)**, I worked on developing advanced LC–Orbitrap metabolomics workflows. The project aimed to enhance metabolite profiling for biomedical and environmental applications, using high-resolution mass spectrometry to obtain accurate and reproducible molecular fingerprints.

Figure 1. Orbitrap LC–MS instrumentation at CUHK for metabolomics studies.

Methods

The workflows combined **LC–Orbitrap mass spectrometry** with optimized calibration, isotopic correction, and compound annotation pipelines. We applied these methods to biological and environmental samples, focusing on untargeted metabolomics and isotope tracing to evaluate metabolic pathways. Collaborative method transfer ensured reproducibility across laboratories.

Key Results

  • Developed high-resolution LC–Orbitrap metabolomics workflows for diverse applications.
  • Improved compound annotation and isotopologue analysis with custom correction models.
  • Demonstrated workflow utility in biomedical metabolomics and environmental sample analysis.

Impact

This collaboration strengthened analytical capabilities at CUHK by implementing reproducible and advanced LC–Orbitrap metabolomics workflows. It also broadened applications of isotope-based metabolomics, bridging environmental and biomedical sciences in Hong Kong research.
